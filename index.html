Abstract

Reconstructing dynamic visual scenes from electroencephalography (EEG) signals presents a significant challenge. Existing methods often yield temporally disjointed and inaccurate visual semantic reconstructions, struggling with poor dynamic timing alignment and lacking the integration of cognitive priors. In neuroscience, the dual-stream theory describes the physiological basis for the generation and transmission of visual neural signals, offering a valuable prior to guide the reconstruction process. To address these challenges, we follow the guidance of dual-stream theory and introduce DynaMind, a model that reconstructs video by jointly modeling neural dynamics and semantic features using three core modules: a Regional-aware Semantic Mapper (RSM), a Temporal-aware Dynamic Aligner (TDA), and a Dual-Guidance Video Reconstructor (DGVR). The RSM models neural pathways to capture detailed semantic information, using regional-aware encoders interconnected via channel-wise multiplicative gating. Meanwhile, the TDA ensures temporal alignment, generating a dynamic latent sequence (blueprint) that corresponds to the original neural recordings. The DGVR uses both semantic and temporal guidance to translate the aligned blueprint into a high-fidelity video reconstruction. On the SEED-DV and CineBrain datasets, DynaMind improves reconstructed video accuracy by 12.5% and 4.28%, respectively. Moreover, it boosts temporal consistency on both datasets, achieving score increases of 19.7% (FVMD) and 4.19% (FVD).

Methodology

DynaMind reconstructs video from EEG with three synergistic modules (Figure 2). The Regional-aware Semantic Mapper (RSM) generates a semantic diffusion prior by processing regional brain patterns with neuromorphic feature interactions (channel-wise gating along dual-stream pathways, multimodal alignment to CLIP spaces, and a diffusion prior for CLIP text conditioning). The Temporal-aware Dynamic Aligner (TDA) builds a coherent temporal blueprint from windowed EEG via a temporal network, aligned to video via frame-wise and structural consistency losses. The Dual-Guidance Video Reconstructor (DGVR) is a latent video diffusion model that initializes latents from the temporal blueprint and injects the semantic prior through cross-attention, yielding reconstructions that are both semantically accurate and temporally coherent.

Results

We evaluate on SEED-DV (40-class EEG-video stimuli) and CineBrain (naturalistic clips). On SEED-DV, RSM features set a new state of the art in EEG classification (Table 1); video reconstruction surpasses EEG2Video across semantic and pixel metrics as the number of classes grows (Table 2), including lower Fréchet Video Motion Distance (FVMD) for smoother temporal dynamics. On CineBrain, our model improves over GLFA and CineSync-EEG on semantic and perceptual scores (Table 3). Ablations (Table 4) show that all brain regions contribute, with the occipital lobe most critical; multimodal features (especially text) and the structural consistency objective are essential for semantic quality and FVMD.

Qualitatively (Figure 4), reconstructions better match ground-truth semantics (e.g., cat, car, river) and exhibit smoother motion with fewer flicker and warping artifacts than EEG2Video.
